💣 BOCCE ROULETTE
Rules · v3.0 · A Hilton Family Game

Objective

First to 40 pts wins. Avg 7–8 rounds. Tiebreak: highest score. Yellow ball is target.

The Deck

One shuffled deck — all 3 card types mixed. Each player draws one per round.

  • Gold Max Challenge — Mandatory. Announce A or B before round.
  • Orange Challenge — Announce accept or decline before round. Removed either way.
  • Green Bomb Card — Say "I have a Bomb Card." Keep power secret. Hold until you play it.
Played = Removed. If deck runs out, reshuffle discarded Orange & Gold back in. Played Bomb cards are permanently gone.

Round Structure

  1. Player A places yellow ball.
  2. Player B picks throwing location.
  3. Player C throws first — all throw 1st ball in turn.
  4. All throw 2nd ball same order.
  5. Two furthest balls each earn one bonus reroll.
  6. Cards resolved at stated timing, then scoring.
  7. Rotate roles clockwise.
Equidistant balls → measure to nearest cm. Still tied → rock-paper-scissors.

Scoring

  • Closest → 3 pts
  • 2nd closest → 2 pts
  • 3rd closest → 1 pt
  • Furthest → 0 pts
Top 2 = your ball is closest or 2nd closest on the field. Ball position — not score.

Bomb Card Rules

  • One per round per player. #94 Nope is always playable as reaction.
  • Announce "I have a Bomb Card" when drawn. Keep power secret.
  • Played or Nope'd = permanently removed from game.

Max Challenge (Gold) Rules

  • Cannot be declined — choose A (bail, lose pts) or B (attempt, big payoff or big penalty).
  • #22: If not in last place — discard and draw again.

Card Conflict Rule

Two cards conflict → higher-numbered card wins. Lower card discarded, no effect, no penalty.

Definitions

  • Footstep = adult heel-to-toe (~12in). Witnessed by all.
  • Opposite-handed = non-dominant hand. Decide before game.
  • Wrist-to-stomach = wrist touches stomach at release.
  • Seated throw = fully seated, both legs forward. No kneeling.
  • Nuclear Bomb Card = cannot be Nope'd.
House Rule: Funniest reasonable interpretation wins. Dad is final referee. Appeals court is closed.
